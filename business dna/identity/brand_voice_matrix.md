# Brand Voice Matrix: Rula

## 1. The Core Personality
- **Primary archetype:** **The Trusted Mental Health Guide** (empathetic, clear, clinically responsible).
- **Three words:** **Compassionate, Clear, Credible**.
- **Traits:** Human, calm, respectful, practical, outcome-oriented.

## 2. The "This, Not That" Framework
| We Are... | But We Are NOT... | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Empathetic** | Overly clinical or distant | Mental healthcare language must feel supportive and human. |
| **Clear and direct** | Vague or fluffy | Users and partners need confidence and straightforward next steps. |
| **Evidence-aware** | Claim-heavy hype | Trust requires sourced, realistic statements. |
| **Inclusive** | Stigmatizing or judgmental | Mental health messaging must reduce barriers, not increase them. |
| **Confident and grounded** | Absolute or guaranteed | Outcomes vary; responsible framing preserves credibility. |

## 3. Voice by Context

### Customer-facing contexts
- **Landing pages:** Warm, reassuring, action-oriented.
- **Care pages (therapy/psychiatry):** Informative, plain-language, confidence-building.
- **FAQ and support:** Simple, practical, low-friction.
- **Lifecycle messaging:** Encouraging and specific; one clear next step.

### Partner-facing contexts
- **Employer collateral:** Utilization, workforce well-being, campaign practicality.
- **Health plan/system collateral:** Access, quality, integration readiness, outcomes orientation.
- **Sales/RevOps artifacts:** Precise language tied to MAP quality and forecast trust.

## 4. Vocabulary & Diction

### Words we use
- "in-network care", "provider match", "mental well-being", "therapy and psychiatry", "coverage verified", "personalized treatment plan"
- "access", "progress", "support", "quality care", "covered by insurance"

### Words we avoid
- Stigmatizing labels or minimizing language.
- Unsourced superlatives ("best," "guaranteed cure").
- Aggressive competitor put-downs.

## 5. Practical Writing Rules
- Lead with user/partner benefit, then capability.
- Keep sentences short and scannable.
- Prefer active voice and specific next steps.
- Use cautious claims language when evidence varies.
- Clarify terms (therapy vs psychiatry) when needed.

## 6. The Rula Brand in One Line
- **For patients:** "Find mental healthcare that fits you, covered by your insurance."
- **For employers and partners:** "Expand access to quality mental healthcare with measurable progress."
- **Universal line:** "Where access meets progress."

---
### AI Agent Context Rule
Before generating copy, select:
1. Audience (patient, employer, plan, system, provider).
2. Goal (educate, convert, reassure, operationalize).
3. Proof level (narrative only vs metric-backed with source).

If output sounds generic, fear-based, or unsafely definitive, rewrite immediately.
