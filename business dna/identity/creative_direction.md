# Creative & Visual Direction: Rula

## 1. Visual Identity & Palette
- **Primary colors:**
  - **Trust Blue:** `#2563EB` (clarity and confidence)
  - **Deep Navy:** `#0F172A` (stability and readability)
- **Secondary accents:**
  - **Progress Green:** `#10B981`
  - **Warm Amber:** `#F59E0B`
  - **Soft Gray:** `#F1F5F9`
  - **White:** `#FFFFFF`
- **Typography:**
  - Headlines: clean sans-serif, high contrast and high legibility.
  - Body: plain language readability first.
  - Technical blocks: monospace for implementation/protocol content.

## 2. Imagery Style (The "Look")

### Patient-facing creative (primary)
- **Mood:** Calm, reassuring, hopeful, non-judgmental.
- **Metaphors:** Guided care journey, matched support, everyday wellbeing progress.
- **Human elements:** Diverse real people in realistic environments; avoid performative stock tropes.
- **Composition:** Clean layouts with clear path-to-action.

### Employer/partner-facing creative
- **Mood:** Professional, outcomes-aware, implementation-ready.
- **Metaphors:** Access expansion, connected care pathways, operational clarity.
- **Human elements:** HR/benefits/clinical leadership in real collaboration settings.
- **Composition:** Structured visuals with proof blocks and clear CTA hierarchy.

## 3. Ad Creative Guidelines

### Demand generation (patients and partners)
- **Formats:** Static social, short video, and carousel with one claim per frame.
- **Formula:** Problem -> credible proof -> practical next step.
- **Always include when true:** Insurance coverage framing, provider fit breadth, access timing.
- **Never include:** Unsourced outcome guarantees or stigmatizing narratives.

### Lifecycle and product comms
- **Formats:** Simple modules, one objective per message.
- **Formula:** Current state -> next action -> expected benefit.
- **Avoid:** Dense claim stacking and jargon-heavy medical language.

## 4. Brand Symbols & Motifs
- **Motif:** "Care path clarity" using connected nodes/steps to represent progression.
- **Iconography:** Clean, minimal icon set around care access, support, and progress.
- **Data visuals:** Prefer simple, sourced proof snippets over complex dashboards in top-funnel creative.

---

### AI Agent Context Rule
When requesting visuals, always specify:
1. Audience (patient/member vs employer vs health plan/system).
2. Message objective (access, cost clarity, outcomes, capacity extension).
3. Allowed claims with source references.

Do not use fear framing, before/after exaggeration, or definitive clinical promises.
