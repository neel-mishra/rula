# Business Context: Rula

## 1. Executive Intent
- **Mission statement:** Make high-quality mental healthcare more accessible, affordable, and personalized.
- **Vision statement:** Build a care platform where people can quickly find in-network therapy and psychiatry that fits their needs, then sustain progress over time.
- **The "Aha" moment:** "I found an in-network provider who fits me, and I can start care quickly without insurance confusion."

## 2. Business Model & Fundamentals
- **Core products:** Insurance-covered therapy, psychiatry/medication management, and integrated care pathways on one platform.
- **Revenue model:** B2B2C marketplace + provider-group model anchored in payer/employer/health-system partnerships and reimbursed patient sessions.
- **Primary customer segments:**
  - **Patients/members:** People seeking online or in-person mental healthcare.
  - **Employers:** Benefits stakeholders trying to improve access/utilization and workforce well-being.
  - **Health plans and health systems:** Organizations needing fast in-network behavioral health capacity and measurable outcomes.
- **Business fundamentals from public claims:**
  - Typical insured session cost communicated as about $15 average.
  - 21,000+ licensed providers and 180+ specialties/modalities.
  - 170M+ individuals covered through insurance network reach.
  - Outcomes claims include meaningful PHQ/GAD improvement within 8 sessions for many patients.

- **Strategic flywheel:** Better match and faster access -> earlier treatment start -> measurable clinical progress -> stronger trust and utilization -> stronger partner value.
- **Differentiation themes:** Insurance-first access, large provider network, care personalization, integrated therapy + psychiatry journey, and partnership-ready operating model.

## 3. Product Portfolio Snapshot
- **Care access layer:** Provider matching by state/city/specialty/method and coverage verification.
- **Clinical service layer:** Individual, couples, family, child, teen therapy, plus psychiatry and medication management.
- **Partner layer:** Employer, health-plan, and health-system partnership programs for scalable access and outcomes alignment.
- **GTM framing for agent system:** "Fast, in-network mental healthcare that drives patient progress and partner value."

## 4. Strategic Priorities
- **Access and speed:** Reduce time from intent to first appointment.
- **Insurance clarity:** Minimize friction around eligibility, benefits, and out-of-pocket expectations.
- **Clinical quality and continuity:** Increase progress rates and care adherence across therapy and psychiatry pathways.
- **Employer-channel performance:** Improve campaign utilization and MAP commitment quality in the employer motion.
- **Evidence and trust:** Maintain transparent, defensible claims with clear source provenance.

## 5. Organizational Context
- **Cross-functional operating requirement:** GTM, RevOps, Product, Clinical, and partner teams need a shared source of truth for messaging and automation.
- **Employer-channel sales reality (from case-study PDF):**
  - Product is free for employers; conversion hinges on campaign commitment and execution, not a paid contract.
  - MAP (Mutual Action Plan) quality directly impacts forecasting and quota trust.
- **Agent-system implication:** Context files must support both prospecting personalization and MAP verification rigor.

## 6. Positioning and Proof Themes
- **Category:** Insurance-covered mental healthcare access and delivery platform (therapy + psychiatry).
- **Core promise:** "Get matched quickly with in-network providers who fit your needs, then make measurable progress."
- **Proof motifs to reuse:**
  - Speed to appointment (as soon as tomorrow).
  - Average insured affordability claims.
  - Provider-network depth and specialty breadth.
  - Patient-reported improvement and clinical progress metrics.
