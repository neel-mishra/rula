# Product DNA: Rula

## 1. Value Proposition

### For Patients and Members
- **Core value prop:** Find in-network mental healthcare that fits your needs quickly, with clear cost expectations and flexible care formats.
- **Jobs to be done:**
  1. **Start care faster:** Reduce delay from need recognition to first appointment.
  2. **Reduce insurance friction:** Understand coverage and expected cost before treatment begins.
  3. **Get better-fit care:** Match with providers by specialty, modality, identity preferences, and care format.

### For Employers, Plans, and Systems
- **Core value prop:** Improve behavioral-health access and outcomes through an in-network, scalable care model with measurable progress signals.
- **Jobs to be done:**
  1. **Increase utilization of covered mental-health benefits.**
  2. **Improve continuity and clinical progress across therapy/psychiatry pathways.**
  3. **Operationalize access at scale without building all capacity in-house.**

## 2. Product Moat (The "Magic")
- **Insurance-first design:** Affordability and coverage workflows are central, not bolted on.
- **Breadth with fit:** Large provider network plus preference-based matching.
- **Integrated care journey:** Therapy and psychiatry available within the same care ecosystem.
- **Partnership-grade operating model:** Employer, plan, and system workflows are explicitly supported.

## 3. Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)

### Patient and Member USPs
| USP | Description | Proof Point |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fast access** | Appointments available quickly, including as soon as tomorrow in many cases | Public access-speed claims on core care pages |
| **Insurance clarity** | Cost estimation and coverage verification before first appointment | Typical insured cost claims and pre-visit estimate language |
| **Provider fit depth** | Large network with many specialties/modalities | 21,000+ provider and 180+ specialty claims |
| **Integrated therapy + psychiatry** | Unified pathway across modalities | Combination-care framing across therapy/psychiatry pages |

### Partner USPs
| USP | Description | Proof Point |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Employer benefit activation** | Helps teams use existing coverage more effectively | Employer partnership framing and utilization narrative |
| **Plan member outcomes** | In-network access with measurable progress orientation | Health-plan partnership outcomes claims |
| **Health-system capacity extension** | Adds mental-health capacity without facility expansion | Health-system partnership positioning |
| **Operational integration** | Platform and workflow integration support for partners | Connected-platform statements on partnership pages |

## 4. Product Design Logic
- **Access drives outcomes:** Faster, lower-friction entry to care improves probability of sustained treatment.
- **Affordability enables adherence:** Insurance clarity supports continuity.
- **Personalization improves engagement:** Better provider match increases trust and retention in care.
- **Operational confidence at partner level:** Clear pathways and evidence improve adoption across employer/plan/system channels.

---
### AI Agent Context Rule
When generating product-facing content, specify:
- **Audience:** patient/member vs employer vs plan/system stakeholder.
- **Primary objective:** access, affordability, quality/progress, or operational scale.
- **Proof style:** sourced claims only (no unsourced guarantees).

Default to practical language over abstract marketing language.
