## 1. Segmentation Axis (Employer Channel)

Primary segmentation is by **organizational type + employee scale + payer alignment + execution readiness**.

| Segment | Baseline criteria | Why it matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health systems** | 3,000+ employees | Strong workforce mental-health need, value-based care overlap, internal referral pathways |
| **Universities** | 3,000+ employees (staff + eligible populations) | High stress populations, programmatic campaign opportunities |
| **Large commercial employers** | Priority-plan aligned where possible | Broader utilization upside and stronger ROI narrative potential |

Secondary filters:
- Contact seniority and ownership of benefits decision.
- Existing channel to employees (email, benefits portal, manager toolkits).
- Campaign-operating willingness across multiple quarters.

---

## 2. Geography

- Public site indicates broad U.S. availability, including all states in care discovery/navigation context.
- Employer prioritization should follow concentration of target account density and payer alignment rather than generic geographic spread.

---

## 3. Product Footprint Relevance by Buyer

- **Employers:** Benefit-utilization and employee access story.
- **Health plans:** In-network access + progress outcomes + member experience.
- **Health systems:** Capacity extension + reduced referral leakage + quality alignment.

For employer AEs, therapy + psychiatry combination messaging is often strongest when framed around speed, continuity, and symptom progress.

---

## 4. Value/Lifetime Logic (Proxy for Prioritization)

Given no employer contract revenue in the case prompt, account value is estimated via utilization proxies:
- Campaign frequency and quarter commitment (MAP depth).
- Reachable covered population and channel execution quality.
- Probability of repeated campaign launches.

Practical scoring dimensions for agents:
1. ICP fit strength.
2. Contact quality and authority.
3. Plan/channel alignment.
4. MAP commitment confidence.

---

## 5. Expansion/Contraction Signals

Expansion indicators:
- Multi-quarter campaign commitment.
- Added campaign modalities (launch email, benefits inserts, manager toolkit).
- Stakeholder broadening beyond a single champion.

Contraction indicators:
- Vague intent language without timeline.
- Single-channel, one-off campaign proposals.
- No confirmation from benefits decision-maker.

---

## 6. Channel Signals (Prospecting and Launch)

High-signal channels in this motion:
- Personalized outbound (email, LinkedIn, phone) to 1-2 senior contacts.
- Discovery meetings tied to segment-specific value narrative.
- MAP planning artifacts and written commitment capture.

---

## 7. Retention Lens for Employer Motion

Retention proxy is not logo churn in this context; it is **campaign continuity**:
- Quarter-over-quarter campaign execution.
- Consistency between planned MAP and observed launches.
- Follow-through from verbal commitment to documented calendar.

---

## 8. Personas by Tier

- **Benefits executive (VP/CPO/HR SVP):** Economic and workforce outcomes, confidence in execution.
- **Benefits manager/director:** Operational feasibility, campaign workflow, support burden.
- **Health-system HR/clinical leadership:** Capacity, access, and quality alignment.

---

## 9. Anti-ICP

- Organizations with low campaign-operating willingness.
- Contacts without decision authority and no path to authority.
- Cases where benefits channel access is blocked or unverifiable.

---

### AI Agent Context Rule
Every prospecting output must include:
1. Segment type and confidence (health system, university, large employer).
2. Top 1-2 value propositions mapped to account signals.
3. MAP readiness estimate and evidence needs.
4. Clear next-step ask tied to campaign calendar progression.

For MAP verification outputs, explicitly separate firm commitments from exploratory language.
